ROBINHOODZ

Rob the rich · Feed the Reserve · Earn $FETA — Whitepaper v1

01ABSTRACT

RobinHoodz is a browser-based, pixel top-down heist game on Robinhood Chain (an Arbitrum Orbit L2) with a real token economy built on one uncompromising principle: $FETA is earned by playing and competing — never taken from another player, and never promised as passive yield. Players rob the rich to build reputation, form gangs, survive the police, climb a season leaderboard, and claim real token payouts at the First Feather Bank — funded entirely by a transparent, on-chain treasury called the Feather-Ral Reserve.

02THE WORLD

New Sherwood, 2031. The banks wrote the rules, then bought the referees. Bank Row forecloses by algorithm; Whale Bay parties on yields nobody downtown will ever see; The Vaults hold more locked wealth than the rest of the city combined. Then somebody anonymous spun up the Feather-Ral Reserve — an unbreakable vault that skims 1% of every $FETA trade and rains it back on the streets. Nobody knows who built it. Everybody knows what it means: robbing the rich finally pays a dividend to the block.

You wear the green hood. Out here your name means nothing — your AURA is your name.

03GAMEPLAY

Players pick one of five hooded outlaws — Marian, Loxley, John, Scarlett, or Ribbit the frog — and work the districts. Robbing bankers and whales earns respect, not cheese: street crime pays AURA only. Real value enters through scarcer, riskier events: the Federal Convoy (a scheduled armored-truck run carrying payroll) and building heists (cracking the banks and vaults themselves at high level, at the cost of maximum police heat).

Heat is skill-based: every patrol projects a visible sight ring, and a robbery committed inside it maxes your heat instantly. Hot players get chased on foot — and caught players take fines and strikes. Three strikes lands you in the interrogation room: take the fall (your rep wiped) or pay to snitch on a rival gang and wear the 🐀 rat tag, bleeding your own gang's standing while the whole city hunts you.

05GANGS & COMPETITION

At level 10, players may form or join gangs (formation fee paid to the Reserve). Gangs boost the emissions each member personally earns — capped at +25% — and unlock raids: pay a fee (to the Reserve), attack a rival crew, and shift REP between crews on a win or loss. The core rule of all player-versus-player systems:

REP moves between players. $FETA never does. Every fee is a Reserve sink; every payout comes from the protocol. No player can take another player's tokens under any mechanic, ever. Competition determines your share of the pool — not access to someone else's wallet.

06SEASONS, LEADERBOARD & GIVEAWAYS

Each season, players are ranked on the street board (individuals, by personal REP) and crews on the gang board (combined crew REP). At the season horn, a slice of the Feather-Ral Reserve becomes the giveaway pool, paid out by rank on a decaying curve — the top eats first, but everyone who competes places. The #1 crew takes an additional champion bonus. Payouts are claimed by players themselves, in-game, at the First Feather Bank.

08ARCHITECTURE — THE TWO-LEDGER MODEL

In-game $FETA is an off-chain scoreboard: it tallies what fraction of the next season pool each player is accruing. On-chain $FETA is the real token. The two meet exactly once per season, through the FetaClaimBank contract:

Season workflow: the leaderboard closes → the Reserve swaps WETH for $FETA → the operator funds the ClaimBank and calls award(winners[], amounts[], season) in a single transaction → each winner presses CLAIM at the First Feather Bank in-game and the contract pays their wallet directly. The contract enforces its own solvency, zeroes balances before transfer (no reentrancy), and cannot pay anyone the operator didn't award.

The game client never holds funds, never mints, and never triggers payouts — so a modified client can cheat a scoreboard at worst, never a wallet.

Wallet accounts register once with a Street License — a small one-time ETH fee paid directly to the Feather-Ral Reserve that marks the wallet payout-eligible for leaderboard ranking and season claims. Guests play free, unranked. In-game fees and amounts are gameplay parameters and are subject to change; current values are always visible in-game before you confirm any transaction.
